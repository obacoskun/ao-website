Electricity

Using the intelligence gathered from consumption data, electric utilities can increase operational efficiency, predict loads and outages and be prepared for various situations by performing what-if analysis.

For over a century, coal and other fossil fuels have been used by power plants to produce the electricity we use everyday.

As technology transforms the electric grid, the rapidly emerging microgrids, intelligent sensors, 2-way communication, digitization, and automation of energy management processes have laid the foundation to not only smart grids but also smart energy.

Natural Gas

Not only a source of comfort in homes and productivity for businesses, natural gas has also become a vital fuel source for electric generation throughout the world.

Natural gas is a valuable commodity and a driving force for global economy. Efficient, clean and intelligent technologies are required to better manage the natural gas production and consumption. As is the case with electricity and water, natural gas consumption data provides the intelligence needed to increase the efficiency of our grids and make sustainable use of this precious, finite resource for future generations.
